## OS scheduling: A1.3.3 Approaches for sceduling

### Multilevel queue scedulign 

- Paritions the ready queue into several seperate queues, each with either own sceduling algorithm and priority level. 

Processes are permentaly assigned to one of these queus based on ceratin *charactersitics*, such as **process type, priority** or **memory requirements**. 

Each queue may use different sceduling algorithms, and the queues themselves are sceduled in a specific order, oftne based on priority. 

- Higher-priority queues are given more CPU time compared to lower-priority queues. 


Advantages:
1) Flexiblity in handling different types of processes 
2) Prioritizes critical and interactive processes, improving responsivness for imporant tasks
3) Different scheduling algorithms can be tailored to the needs of eahc queue 

Disadvantages: 
1) Starvation of lower-priority proceeses if higher-priority queues are frequently occupied 
2) Complex implementaiton and management 
3) Processes are permenantly assigned to quese which might not be opitmal if their behavior changes over time

- What is the purpose of priority sceduling, and how does it differ from multilevel queue sceduling? provide an example of where each might be preffered 

The purpose of priority sceduling is to ensure **critical** processes are exectued as soon as possible. This is similar to multilevel queue sceduling, except rather than individual processes being assigned a priority level, queues are assigned a priority level that processes will be sorted within. The sceduling algorithm within each queue may be different based on the types of processes. An example for priority sceduling being preffered would be in emergency medical systems, where based on patient criticality, they are given attention to medical and hospital staff. Whereas for Multilevel sceduling, its utilized for foreground and background systems of a desktop. Here there are two queues, one for background tasks done by the desktop, while the other processes user tasks. 

The focus for priority scheduling is critically for a specific task or critical task. While multilevel queue scheduling is for faster response time requirements for task classes.

- In the context of OS, what is meant by context switching and why is it important in process scheduling 

Context switching is when a CPU pre-empts a process to another, this involves saving the state of the current task and loading the state of a new task. This is imporants as it allows for multitasking effectively, and managing multiple programs within interupptions.

Multilevel queue sceduling works through the usage of multiple queues of processes, executed through an assigned priority. This provides an advantage of felixibility in handling a large variety of processes, allowing for critical tasks to be prioritized and allowing multiple different scheduling algorithms to be utilized within each queue, providing more flexibility and time effectivness based on the process.
